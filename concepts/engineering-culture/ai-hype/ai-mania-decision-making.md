# AI Mania Is Eviscerating Global Decision-Making

The article discusses how the current AI hype is leading to poor decision-making in organizations. Executives are making strategic decisions centered on AI without even using the tools themselves, as evidenced by an executive who produced an AI-centric technical strategy for a $2B+ company despite never using ChatGPT or any AI tool. Engineers feel pressured to rewrite entire codebases in trendy languages like Zig to appear AI-proficient and keep their jobs. The article also reveals a perverse dynamic where vendors cannot contradict their customers' unrealistic claims about AI productivity gains (e.g., 100x) because doing so would undermine the customer's executives and risk contract cancellations.

- Executives create AI-centered strategies without personal experience with AI tools, leading to misinformed decisions.
- Engineers engage in unnecessary technical rewrites (e.g., Go to Zig) to signal AI competence for job security.
- Vendors are incentivized to remain silent about unrealistic AI productivity claims to avoid upsetting customers and losing contracts.
- AI hype distorts honest discourse and constrains critical evaluation of AI's actual capabilities.
