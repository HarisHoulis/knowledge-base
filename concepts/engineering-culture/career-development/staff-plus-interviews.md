# Interviewing at Staff+ Level

Interviewing for Staff+ Individual Contributor positions requires a multifaceted preparation strategy covering four common interview types: Data Structures and Algorithms (DS/A), area of expertise (e.g., Android), system design, and behavioral interviews. For DS/A, practice is key—use resources like 'Cracking the Coding Interview' and platforms like LeetCode, and focus on problem-solving structure: listen, think of edge cases, brute force, optimize, walk through, implement, and test (Vivo, 2024). For system design, especially in mobile, resources like Alex Lementuev's 'Mobile System Design' GitHub project and the ByteByteGo newsletter are invaluable; start by gathering functional and non-functional requirements, then make informed choices about network protocols, data models, and architecture (Vivo, 2024). Behavioral interviews require a structured approach: prepare a table of key projects, reflect on challenges, learnings, conflicts, and business impact, and always be honest while highlighting personal contributions (Vivo, 2024). Throughout, active listening and collaboration with interviewers are critical, as is leveraging leadership books like 'The Staff Engineer's Path' by Tanya Reilly to refine one's leadership style (Vivo, 2024).

- Staff+ interviews typically include DS/A, area expertise, system design, and behavioral components; preparation must be systematic and practice-intensive.
- For system design, ask logical questions upfront to gather requirements, discuss trade-offs, and use mobile-specific resources like the Mobile System Design GitHub project.
- Behavioral interviews should focus on expertise, leadership, and impact—prepare examples of business impact, acting as a multiplier, and thinking about business outcomes.
- Honesty is essential; sell yourself but never lie, and adapt lessons from leadership books to your authentic style.
- Plan time across interview types (e.g., weeks per topic) and conduct mock interviews to improve.
