# The Lysine Contingency

Retrofit, OkHttp, Okio, and SQL Delight are moving to a new GitHub organization and joining the Commonhaus Foundation, reinforcing their commitment to long-term project stewardship. The move comes after the projects' founders, Jake Wharton and Jesse Wilson, left Square in November 2023, and the company did not maintain good-faith stewardship. The 'lysine contingency' is a reference to Jurassic Park, symbolizing a plan to limit damage from the old company. The projects are now free to live and thrive independently, with possible future additions.

- Retrofit, OkHttp, Okio, and SQL Delight are moving to lysine.dev and joining Commonhaus Foundation.
- The move ensures project longevity beyond a single company's control.
- Founders Jake Wharton and Jesse Wilson quit Square in November 2023 due to loss of trust in the company's stewardship.
- The 'lysine contingency' metaphor describes the plan to protect open source projects from corporate damage.
- The founders express gratitude to contributors and look forward to continued collaboration at the new home.
