# The Pragmatic Engineer AMA

In this special episode, Gergely Orosz shares his journey from Uber engineer to writer, revealing that the COVID-19 pandemic catalyzed his shift to full-time writing. He recounts how a personal message from an engineer changed his editorial policy to focus on what works inside companies rather than exposés, though he did publish one investigative piece on Pollen's collapse after being dismissed by its CEO. Orosz offers opinions on LeetCode interviews, arguing they persist because they self-select candidates tolerant of meaningless corporate tasks. He also discusses how the MCP protocol became an industry standard due to Anthropic's non-dominant position at launch, and cautions that AI adoption can lead to skill atrophy, urging engineers to deliberately maintain skills they value.

- Orosz's editorial policy prioritizes writing about what works in companies, influenced by a grateful Bunq engineer who appreciated the opportunity despite a tough work environment.
- He believes LeetCode-style interviews survive because they filter for candidates willing to tolerate pointless work, a trait that serves them well in big tech.
- MCP became standard partly because Anthropic wasn't seen as a dominant threat in late 2024; today such a protocol might face adoption resistance.
- AI tools can cause skill atrophy; Orosz avoids AI in writing to preserve his skill, but accepts its use in coding will degrade his hand-coding ability.
