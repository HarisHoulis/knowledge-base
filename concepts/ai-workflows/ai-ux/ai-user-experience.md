# The UX of AI: Making AI-Powered Apps Your Users Don't Hate

The talk addresses the critical UX challenges in AI-powered applications. The speaker, Kathryn Grayson Nanz, highlights the widening knowledge gap between developers and users as AI introduces non-deterministic outputs and novel interaction patterns. Users are often presented with AI features they don't understand, leading to frustration and disengagement. The speaker draws an analogy to the evolution of the Macintosh GUI, which initially used real-world metaphors and gradually introduced more technical terms as users became literate. Currently, AI is at an early stage ("system 3") where developers must use familiar abstractions and walk users through features to build trust and understanding. Key strategies include borrowing vocabulary from real life, providing visual cues, and evolving the UX as users gain experience.

- AI's non-deterministic nature (varied quality, format, accuracy) creates UX challenges that require new interaction patterns.
- Users have limited patience; repeated poor AI experiences cause disengagement, so developers must get the UX right early.
- The Macintosh GUI evolution shows how to gradually introduce new technology: start with real-world metaphors and simplify, then increase complexity as users become literate.
- Currently, AI UX is at an early stage: designers should avoid expecting users to be experts and instead use familiar abstractions and onboarding flows.
